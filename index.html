Further Reading: A Select Bibliography
  • Edutopia (2023). 7 Classroom Management Mistakes—and the Research on How to Fix Them.

    An excellent article that synthesizes recent research into practical advice for proactive, relationship-centered classroom management.

  • Brown, P. C., Roediger, H. L., & McDaniel, M. A. (2014). Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning.

    This book is the best summary of the cognitive science behind *why* strategies like retrieval practice and spaced repetition are so effective.

  • Lang, J. M. (2021). Small Teaching: Everyday Lessons from the Science of Learning (2nd Ed.).

    This book excels at translating cognitive science into small, practical, high-impact strategies that teachers can implement immediately without overhauling their course.

  • Ritchhart, R., Church, M., & Morrison, K. (2011). Making Thinking Visible: How to Promote Engagement, Understanding, and Independence for All Learners.

    The definitive guide for techniques that make student learning processes visible, featuring dozens of ready-to-use "thinking routines."

  • Wiggins, G., & McTighe, J. (2005). Understanding by Design.

    This foundational text introduces "Backward Design," an approach essential for effectively implementing real-world problem solving by focusing on the end goal first.

  • Lemov, D. (2021). Teach Like a Champion 3.0.

    A toolkit of highly specific, actionable techniques for classroom engagement, including methods for positive reinforcement and active listening.

  • Aronson, E. (1978). The Jigsaw Classroom.

    Written by the inventor of the Jigsaw method, this book explains the methodology and powerful social benefits of one of the best cooperative learning strategies.
